## ğŸ“Š When to Use Agile?

## ğŸ¯ The 12 Principles of Agile

### Principle 1: Customer Satisfaction

```
"Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer

```

**Concept**:
- **Frequent** delivery
- Each delivery has **value**
- Customer sees results **early**

---

### Principle 2: Welcome Changing Requirements

```
"Welcome changing requirements, even late in development.
Agile processes harness change for the customer's competitive advantage"
```

**Concept**:
- Change = **opportunity**, not problem
- Even if late = **we welcome it**
- Quick adaptation = competitive advantage

**Practical Example**:
- Spent $7,500 of $100,000
- Change in requirements came
- With Agile: Stop and modify = loss of only $7,500
- With Waterfall: Complete wrong project = loss of $100,000

---

### Principle 3: Deliver Working Software Frequently

```
"Deliver working software frequently,
from a couple of weeks to a couple of months, with preference to shorter timescale"
```

**Concept**:
- Frequent delivery
- **Two weeks to two months** = ideal range
- Some teams: one week
- Most important: **Consistent Pace**

---

### Principle 4: Daily Cooperation

```
"Business people and developers
must work together daily throughout the project"
```

**Concept**:
- Business people + Developers = **daily collaboration**
- No separation between "management" and "technical"
- Continuous communication = better understanding

---

### Principle 5: Motivated Individuals

```
"Build projects around motivated individuals.
Give them the environment and support they need, and trust them to get the job done"
```

**Concept**:
- Motivated team = higher productivity
- Trust > Surveillance
- Support > Control

---

### Principle 6: Face-to-Face Conversation

```
"The most efficient and effective method of

```

**Concept**:
- Face-to-face > Email
- Direct conversation is faster and clearer
- Body language matters

---

### Principle 7: Working Software

```
"Working software is the primary measure of progress"
```

**Concept**:
- Working product = real progress
- Not documentation or meetings
- Value is in usable result

---

### Principle 8: Sustainable Development

```
"Agile processes promote sustainable development.

**Concept**:
- **Sustainable pace**
- No team burnout
- Continuity > temporary speed

---

### Principle 9: Technical Excellence

```
"Continuous attention to technical excellence

**Concept**:

### Principle 10: Simplicity

**Concept**:

### 1. Scrum - Most Popular Worldwide:

**Description**:
- Most widely used framework
- Suitable for medium and large projects
- Relies on small teams (5-9 people)

**Characteristics**:
- **Sprints** (time-boxed cycles 2-4 weeks)
- **Defined roles** (Scrum Master, Product Owner, Team)
- **Regular meetings** (Daily Standup, Sprint Planning, etc.)

---

### 2. Kanban:

**Description**:
- Focuses on workflow
- Uses Kanban Board
- Visual and easy to understand

**Characteristics**:
- **Columns**: To Do, In Progress, Done
- **WIP Limit** (Work In Progress limit)
- Continuous workflow improvement

---

### 3. Lean:

**Description**:
- Originated from Toyota Japan
- Focuses on **eliminating waste**
- Maximize value

**Principles**:
- Eliminate Waste
- Build Quality In
- Create Knowledge
- Defer Commitment
- Deliver Fast

---

### 4. Kaizen:

**Description**:
- Japanese word meaning **continuous improvement**
- Kai = Change / Zen = Good
- Philosophy of small continuous improvements

---

### 5. Scrumban:

**Description**:
- Mix of **Scrum + Kanban**
- Takes the best of both
- Greater flexibility
