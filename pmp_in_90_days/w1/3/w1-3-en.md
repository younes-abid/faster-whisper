## üéØ Quick Review of Core Concepts

### Project
- Temporary endeavor
- To produce a unique product, service, or result
- Has a definite beginning and end

### Program
- A group of **related projects**
- Managed in a coordinated manner
- To achieve **shared benefits**
- Projects are interconnected
- Share: resources, budget, objectives

### Portfolio
- A collection of programs and projects
- May include **independent projects**
- Also includes **ongoing operations**
- Aligned with **strategic objectives**

## üìä Difference Between Program and Portfolio - Riyadh Metro Example

### Portfolio:
- **Entire Riyadh Metro** = portfolio
- Aligned with **Vision 2030** (strategic objective)
- Includes multiple programs

### Program:
- **Stations Program** = group of related projects
- **Lines Program** = group of related projects
- **Civil Works Program** = group of related projects
- Each program has a **Program Manager**

### Project:
- **Blue Line Project** = single project
- **KAFD Station Project** = single project
- Each project has a **Project Manager**

## üéì Practical Exam Questions

### Example Question 1:

**Question**: 
James was hired to lead a temporary endeavor to deliver or produce a change or business value.

- **Answer**: James is leading a **Project**
- **Explanation**: The phrase "temporary endeavor" indicates a project

---

### Example Question 2:

**Question**:
Alex is leading a group of related projects that are managed as a group.

- **Answer**: Alex is leading a **Program**
- **Explanation**: "Related projects managed as a group" = program

---

### Example Question 3:

**Question**:
Rachel is leading a collection of projects and operations to achieve a strategic objective.

- **Answer**: Rachel is leading a **Portfolio**
- **Explanation**: 
  - Presence of "operations"
  - "Strategic objective"
  - Projects may be independent

## üèõÔ∏è PMO Types - Review with Examples

### 1. Supportive PMO
- **Control**: Low
- **Role**: Support and consultation only
- **Example**: Provides templates, documentation, and best practices

### 2. Controlling PMO
- **Control**: Moderate
- **Role**: Governance and monitoring
- **Compliance**: Requires adherence to standards and frameworks
- **Example**: Specifies a framework (Waterfall or Agile) and requires compliance

### 3. Directive PMO
- **Control**: High
- **Role**: Direct management
- **Authority**: Assigns project managers
- **Reporting**: Project managers report directly to PMO director
- **Example**: Directly manages projects with full authority

## üéØ Key Takeaways

1. ‚úÖ **Program** contains **related** projects that are interconnected
2. ‚úÖ **Portfolio** may contain **independent** projects that are not related
3. ‚úÖ **Portfolio only** includes operations
4. ‚úÖ **Portfolio** is aligned with **long-term strategic** objectives
5. ‚úÖ In a **Program**: Projects share resources and budget
6. ‚úÖ Each **Program** has one Program Manager overseeing multiple Project Managers
7. ‚úÖ **Directive PMO** has the highest level of control and authority
8. ‚úÖ Riyadh Metro is an excellent practical example of a **large portfolio** with multiple **programs**

## üí° Exam Tips

### How to Distinguish Between Portfolio and Program in Questions?

#### If you see in the question:
- ‚úÖ **"Strategic Objective"** = Portfolio
- ‚úÖ **"Operations"** or **"Ongoing Work"** = Portfolio
- ‚úÖ **"Independent Projects"** = Portfolio
- ‚úÖ **"Related Projects"** = Program
- ‚úÖ **"Shared Resources/Benefits"** = Program
- ‚úÖ **"Temporary Endeavor"** = Project

### Keywords:
- **Portfolio** ‚Üí Strategic, independent, operations
- **Program** ‚Üí Related, shared benefits, shared resources
- **Project** ‚Üí Temporary, unique, specific deliverables
