### 3.3 Balanced Matrix

#### Characteristics:

- **Middle ground** between Weak and Strong
- **Project Manager**: **Moderate authority**
- **Resources**: Shared between PM and functional managers
- **Reporting**: Staff may report to both

#### Typical Scenario:

  - Same staff works with **Project Manager B** 3 days/week

#### Role of PMO:

**Project Management Office (PMO)** acts as **mediator**:

- Takes **calendar/schedule** from each Project Manager
- Knows when each project needs resources
- **Coordinates** resource sharing between projects
- Example:
  - Experienced maintenance staff
  - PMO knows all project schedules
  - Decides: "From date X to Y works with Project A"
  - "From date Y to Z works with Project B"

#### Challenges:

- **Resource conflicts**
- One employee working with multiple PMs
- Requires **precise coordination** from PMO or senior management

## ğŸ’¡ Exam Tips

### Situational Questions:

The exam will ask situational questions like:

#### Question Type 1: "PM needs resources..."

**Question**: 
"Project Manager needs an engineer for 2 weeks, but the **functional manager refused**. What should the PM do?"

**Analysis**:
- If **functional manager refused** = PM has **limited authority**
- Answer: **Weak Matrix** or **Functional**

---

#### Question Type 2: "PM makes decisions..."

**Question**:
"Project Manager decided to **increase budget** and **hire 3 new engineers** without consulting anyone. What type of structure?"

**Analysis**:
- PM makes **independent decisions**
- **Very high authority**
- Answer: **Projectized** or **Strong Matrix**

---

#### Question Type 3: "Employee reports to two..."

**Question**:
"Ahmad works as an accountant in Finance department, and also works 3 days/week with Project Manager. Reports to **both**. What structure?"

**Analysis**:
- Employee works with **department** and **project**
- Reports to **two people**
- Answer: **Balanced Matrix**

### Example 1: Software Company

**Scenario**:

- Developers work in Development department

### Example 2: Hospital

**Scenario**:
Hospital wants to implement **electronic medical records system**

**Current Structure**: Functional (departments: ER, Surgery, Nursing, IT, Admin)

**Implementation Options**:

1. **Weak Matrix**:
   - Assign coordinator from IT
   - Coordinator tries to coordinate with departments
   - But each department has its priorities
   - **Result**: Project delays significantly

2. **Balanced Matrix**:
   - Assign real Project Manager
   - Allocate IT staff part-time
   - PMO coordinates resources
   - **Result**: Good balance

3. **Strong Matrix or Projectized**:
   - Fully dedicated project team
   - Full authority to PM
   - **Result**: Fast execution
