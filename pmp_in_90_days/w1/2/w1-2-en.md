## üîÑ Differences Between Portfolio, Programs, and Projects

### 1. Project

**Definition**: A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result.

**Characteristics**:
- Temporary (has beginning and end)
- Unique (doesn't repeat identically)
- Smallest unit in the hierarchy

**Example**: Building a specific factory, developing a new product, establishing a production line

---

### 2. Program

**Definition**: A group of related projects managed in a coordinated way to obtain benefits not available from managing them individually.

#### Characteristics:
- Consists of **several related projects**
- Projects are **interconnected**
- They share:
  - Objectives
  - Expected benefits
  - Resources (human and material)
  - Budget
  - Authority

**Real-Life Example**:
- PROTON Manufacturing Program:
  - Project 1: Discontinue old model production line
  - Project 2: Add production line for new model (Perdana)
  - Project 3: Develop new car model (Saga)
  - All under one manufacturing program

---

### 3. Portfolio

**Definition**: A collection of programs, projects, and operations managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives.

#### Characteristics:
- Consists of **multiple programs** and/or **independent projects**
- May contain **operational work**
- **Projects may be unrelated** (independent of each other)
- Aligned with **strategic objectives** of the organization

**Hierarchical Structure**:

## üíº Case Study: PROTON Malaysia

### Background:

#### About the Company:
- **PROTON**: Malaysian government-owned automotive company
- **Founded**: 1980s
- **Founder**: Dr. Mahathir Mohamad (former Prime Minister of Malaysia)

#### Situation in 2015:
- **Market share**: Dropped to only **25%**
- **Reason**: Competition from Perodua and foreign companies
- Competitors offered better quality and value

---

### Strategic Solution: Creating a Portfolio to Increase Market Share

#### Strategic Objective:
Increase market share from **25%** to **40%**

#### Portfolio: "Market Share Growth Portfolio"

This portfolio consists of several programs:

---

### üì¶ Program 1: Manufacturing Program

#### Projects under this program:

1. **Old Model Discontinuation Project**
   - Shut down production line for outdated car model

2. **New Production Line Addition Project**
   - Manufacture new model (Perdana)

3. **New Car Development Project**
   - Develop Saga model

4. **Quality Improvement Project**
   - Improve quality of existing products

**Program Objective**: Modernize production lines and introduce new models

---

### üë• Program 2: Human Resources Program

#### Projects under this program:

1. **Talent Acquisition Project**
   - Recruit engineers and specialists

2. **Training and Development Project**
   - Train employees on new technologies

3. **Employee Motivation Project**
   - Rewards and incentives programs

**Program Objective**: Build a strong, qualified workforce

---

### üì¢ Program 3: Marketing Program

#### Projects under this program:

1. **TV Advertising Project**
   - Television advertising campaigns

2. **Mall and Market Marketing Project**
   - Exhibitions and kiosks in public places

3. **Newspaper Advertising Project**
   - Print advertisements (common in 2015)

4. **Digital Marketing Project**
   - Online and social media advertising

**Program Objective**: Increase brand awareness and attract customers

---

### Result:
- Portfolio = Market Share Growth
  - Program 1: Manufacturing (several projects)
  - Program 2: Human Resources (several projects)
  - Program 3: Marketing (several projects)

All programs and projects work together to achieve the strategic goal: **Increase market share from 25% to 40%**

## üèõÔ∏è Project Management Office (PMO)

### What is a PMO?

**PMO** = Project Management Office

**Definition**: A centralized organizational unit to standardize project management practices.

---

### Types of PMO:

#### 1. Supportive PMO
- **Role**: Provides support and consultation
- **Control**: Low - support only
- Provides:
  - Documentation and templates
  - Policies and procedures
  - Consultation when needed
  - Best practices

#### 2. Controlling PMO
- **Role**: Governance and oversight
- **Control**: Moderate
- Ensures:
  - Compliance with standards
  - Good governance
  - Regular monitoring and reports

#### 3. Directive PMO
- **Role**: Direct project management
- **Control**: High - full control
- Handles:
  - Direct project management
  - Assigns project managers
  - Complete control over resources and decisions

---

### PMO Responsibilities:

1. **Provide Documentation and Templates**
   - Ready-to-use forms
   - Policies and procedures

2. **Governance**
   - Ensure compliance with standards
   - Oversee project progress

3. **Support and Consultation**
   - Assist project managers
   - Provide technical advice

4. **Resource Management**
   - Distribute resources among projects
   - Manage human resources

5. **Reporting and Monitoring**
   - Project managers report to PMO
   - PMO reports to senior management

---

### Practical Example:

#### PMO in Malaysia:
- **National Level**: PMO office under the Prime Minister
- **23 types of PMO** in various ministries and government agencies
- Oversees all major government projects

## üìä Key Differences

### Comparison Table:

| Element | Project | Program | Portfolio |
|---------|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Definition** | Temporary work for unique output | Related projects group | Collection of programs/projects |
| **Scope** | Limited and defined | Broader than project | Strategic - broadest scope |
| **Duration** | Short to medium | Medium to long | Long - strategic |
| **Relationship** | Independent | Related projects | May be independent |
| **Objective** | Specific deliverables | Shared benefits | Strategic objectives |
| **Resources** | Project-specific | Shared among projects | Organization-wide |
